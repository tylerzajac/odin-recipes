Mac and Cheese

Description

This mac and cheese recipe begins with a rich, creamy roux made from melted butter and all-purpose flour whisked together over medium heat. Once the mixture turns golden and fragrant, whole milk is gradually poured in, creating a smooth base. Shredded sharp cheddar and a touch of mozzarella are slowly stirred into the warm sauce until melted and velvety. The cheese sauce is then combined with freshly boiled elbow macaroni, ensuring each piece is thoroughly coated in cheesy goodness.

For a baked version, the mixture is transferred to a greased baking dish and topped with a crunchy layer of breadcrumbs mixed with a bit of Parmesan cheese. It’s then baked in the oven until the top is golden brown and bubbly. The result is a perfectly balanced mac and cheese — creamy on the inside with a crispy, savory topping. Ideal for a comforting main course or a hearty side dish, this recipe delivers classic homemade flavor with simple ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Butter
  • Sweet Onion
  • Heavy Cream
  • Sharp Cheddar Cheese
  • Mozzarella Cheese
  • Elbow Macoroni

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat, whisk in flour to form a roux, then slowly add milk. Stir constantly until thickened, then mix in shredded cheddar and mozzarella until smooth and creamy.
  2. Boil elbow macaroni in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Stir the cooked macaroni into the cheese sauce, making sure all pasta is well-coated.
  4. Pour into a greased baking dish, top with breadcrumbs and Parmesan, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20 minutes or until golden and bubbling.
